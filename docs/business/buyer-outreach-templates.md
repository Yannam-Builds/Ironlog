# Buyer Outreach Template Pack

Use these as starting points. Keep them short, specific, and grounded in what the buyer already understands.

## 1. Short cold outreach email

Subject:

`Android fitness app acquisition opportunity - IRONLOG`

Body:

Hi [Name],

I built IRONLOG, a premium Android workout tracking app focused on fast logging, muscle analytics, recovery maps, adaptive targets, and local-first data ownership.

I am exploring either:

- a full product acquisition
- a commercial license / white-label deal
- or a strategic partnership

This is a better fit for a buyer with existing fitness distribution than for a passive-code marketplace buyer. It already has a serious product foundation, polished UI, export/backup trust features, and a strong Android-first positioning.

If this is relevant, I can send:

- a short demo
- screenshots
- feature summary
- valuation range
- and what would be included in a full transfer

Best,
[Your name]

## 6. Reply when someone asks, "What makes it different?"

The short answer is that IRONLOG is built more like a serious training product than a simple logbook.

Key differences:

- fast in-session logging
- adaptive next-session targets
- muscle analytics and recovery maps
- local-first backup/export trust layer
- polished premium Android presentation

It is much closer to a real commercial product foundation than to a code template.

## 7. Reply when someone asks, "What are you asking?"

I am open to a few structures depending on fit:

- full acquisition
- exclusive commercial license
- non-exclusive commercial license
- white-label arrangement

For a full acquisition, the current expected range depends on what is included and whether the buyer values it as a near-launch product asset versus a pure code purchase.

## 8. Reply when someone asks, "Why sell?"

I am open to selling because the product may create more value faster with a buyer that already has distribution, customers, or a larger operating footprint in fitness.

That said, I am also prepared to launch it myself, so I am not looking for a distressed or rushed exit.

## 9. Questions to ask a buyer early

- Are you looking for a full acquisition, license, or partnership?
- Do you care more about the codebase, the product, or the brand?
- Would you want branding included?
- Are you buying for your existing audience, a white-label offer, or a portfolio roll-up?
- What timeline are you working on?
